About Us

Namaste!

India Association (IA) is one of the most active Recognized Student Organizations (RSO) at Missouri S & T. Our mission is primarily focused on helping the Indian student community while nurturing the culture & traditions of India. We have always believed in cherishing our diverse Indian roots & culture. At IA, Indian festivals are more than just events. They are an expression of our Indian tradition and diversity. We help new students gel in with our closely knit community, and we welcome you to join our extended family halfway around the world from home.

India Association helps all incoming students with an airport pick up service along with temporary accommodation, until they find a permanent place to stay. IA also provides pick-up and drop services to Lambert International Airport at St. Louis during the Thanksgiving, spring, winter & summer holiday breaks.

IA’s finest contribution to the Missouri S & T community takes place twice every academic year for conducting an event for Diwali & Holi. These are some of the biggest shows that take place on campus each year, where IA members display an amazing array of talent in different art performances & culinary skills! (Yes, students cook dinner for more than 800+ people who attend these events.)

Check back on our website for regular updates about our events & other India related news!

Diwali Celebrations

Saturday, October 22, 2016

India Association at Missouri S&T, will host a celebration of Diwali later this month.

The celebration will be held Saturday, Oct. 22, and will include a cultural show and an authentic Indian dinner.

The cultural show will be held 4-6 p.m. in Leach Theatre of Castleman Hall, 10th and Main streets, and will feature authentic Indian dances, plays and a performance of western and Indian music. A fireworks display will be held at 6:15 p.m. at the University Promenade, the walkway between the Havener Center and Engineering Management buildings on the S&T campus.

The dinner starts from 6:30 p.m. in St. Pat’s Ballroom of the Havener Center on the S&T campus.

Tickets are $4 for the cultural show and $10 for the dinner and can be purchased 11 a.m. to 3 p.m. starting Oct. 10 in the Havener Center atrium and in front of Curtis Laws Wilson Library.

Diwali, one of India’s most vibrant festivals, is popularly known as the festival of lights and represents the triumph of good over evil. The word Diwali is derived from the Sanskrit word “Deepavali” meaning row of lamps.
